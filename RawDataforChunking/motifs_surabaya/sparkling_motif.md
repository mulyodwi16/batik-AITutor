# Sparkling Motif - Taste of Surabaya

## Origin

Created by Arina Halimatul Anjani, this batik motif is inspired by the diversity of arts, culture, and cuisine unique to the city of Surabaya, combining two key cultural elements into a unified expression.

## Visual Characteristics

The motif integrates influences from traditional dance with culinary visual elements, creating a dynamic and energetic design that celebrates Surabaya's rich cultural and culinary traditions through batik artistry.

## Meaning and Philosophy

The selection of inspirational sources—Sparkling Dance and Rujak Cingur—reflects and represents the spirit and semangat (essence) of Arek Suroboyo (Surabayan people). This spirit originates from the rich diversity of arts and cuisine within Surabayan society, showcasing how cultural and culinary traditions together embody the identity and values of the community.

## Cultural Context

The motif draws from two significant cultural expressions:

**Sparkling (Dance Element)**
The word "Sparkling" is derived from Surabaya's distinctive dance art form: Tari Sparkling (Sparkling Dance). This dance represents a fusion of modern and traditional dance movements, symbolizing beauty, spirit, and the essence of Arek Suroboyo. The dance is characterized by nimble, graceful movements that are rich with aesthetic beauty.

**Taste (Culinary Element)**
The word "Taste" (referring to flavor/palate) is inspired by Surabaya's signature dish: Rujak Cingur. This traditional delicacy consists of vegetables, tofu, tempeh, and cingur (cow's nose), which is beloved by the people of Surabaya. The dish represents local culinary heritage and community taste.

This motif celebrates how Surabayan identity is woven through artistic expression and culinary practice, representing the spirit of vibrancy and resilience that characterizes the city and its people.
