# Kintir Kintiran Motif

## Origin

Created by Nuraini Farida, this batik motif is inspired by Surabaya's natural geographical features, particularly the rivers and water systems that flow through the city.

## Visual Characteristics

The design incorporates three main ornamental elements:
- **Jagged Line Ornament**: Represents the natural richness of mangrove ecosystems with their sharp, intertwined roots
- **Suro and Boyo Ornaments**: Symbolize Arek Suroboyo spirit, with the semanggi (water clover) plant representing love and harmony
- **Bamboo Line Ornaments**: Symbolize struggle (perjuangan) and loyalty (loyalitas)

## Meaning and Philosophy

This batik motif is named to represent Surabaya, which is surrounded by several rivers with abundant and clean natural water resources free from pollution. It symbolizes that Arek Suroboyo (the spirit of Surabayans) is like flowing water that can adapt wherever the tide of progress flows. In essence, the people of Surabaya can endure any condition to pursue their livelihood and aspirations.

## Cultural Context

The original inspiration comes from the rivers flowing through the city of Surabaya, including mangrove ecosystems with all their advantages and natural richness. Arek Suroboyo, known for their toughness, possess loyalty and love that harmonizes with their spirit of struggle (semangat berjuang). This motif celebrates the adaptability, resilience, and industriousness of Surabayan people as they navigate life's challenges with determination and hope.
