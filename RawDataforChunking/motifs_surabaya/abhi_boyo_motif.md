# Abhi Boyo Motif

## Origin

Created by Heri Supriyanto, the Abhi Boyo motif draws from Sanskrit etymology and Surabayan historical narrative, representing a heroic ideal embodied in the city's cultural identity.

## Visual Characteristics

The design combines three key cultural and visual elements: a stylized crocodile (boyo) representing a formidable enemy, parang (diagonal slash) patterns symbolizing resistance, and interlocking geometric forms that suggest continuous connection and unbroken bonds.

## Meaning and Philosophy

The name "Abhi Boyo" derives from Sanskrit: "Abhi Manyu," where:
- **Abhi** = Berani / Pemberani (Brave / Courageous)
- **Manyu** = Tabiat (Nature / Character)

An Abhi Manyu represents someone who is fearless and embodies heroic qualities. This person possesses the following characteristics:
- Refined and gentle demeanor (halus)
- Virtuous conduct and good behavior
- Clear and honest speech (ucapannya terang)
- Strong and resolute heart (hatinya keras)
- Great sense of responsibility
- Courageous and brave spirit

### Symbolic Elements

**Buaya (Crocodile)**
The crocodile (Boyo) represents a fierce and frightening creature—a formidable enemy that threatens life and safety. It symbolizes the grave dangers and challenges one must confront.

**Parang Motif Elements**
The Parang motif within the design conveys a message to never surrender, much like the ocean waves that never cease their movement. It represents perpetual resistance and unending determination.

**Interlocking Motif**
The continuous, unbroken interlocking patterns symbolize:
- Efforts at self-improvement (upaya memperbaiki diri)
- The struggle for communal welfare and prosperity
- The bonds and interconnectedness of family (bentuk pertalian keluarga)

## Cultural Context

### Historical and Cultural Narrative

This motif tells a profound historical story: A young person with refined and virtuous qualities faces an enemy as strong and terrifying as a crocodile. Originally, the region was peaceful, cool, and serene. However, territorial disputes arise, leading to conflict and death.

### Connection to Arek Suroboyo

The young people of Surabaya, embodying the same brave and unwavering spirit, rose to defend their territory against the invading Tar-Tar forces. Despite facing formidable danger, Raden Wijaya was protected and ultimately survived the assault and threats.

### Surabaya's Birthday

The day of this victorious struggle was commemorated as Surabaya's birthday, celebrated on May 31. This date represents not only a military victory but also the eternal spirit of Arek Suroboyo—brave, steadfast, and willing to sacrifice for their homeland and community.

The Abhi Boyo motif thus embodies the historical courage and contemporary values of Surabayan people, serving as a reminder of their heroic heritage and enduring character.
