# Kembang Bungur Motif

## Origin

Created by Risha Iffatur Rahmah, this motif celebrates the Bungur flower (also known as Ketangi, Laban, or Wungu), a plant that is part of Indonesia's native flora and commonly found throughout Surabaya.

## Visual Characteristics

The Bungur flower motif features the distinctive shape of the Bungur blossom. The wavy petals of the flower calyx, supported by the delicate stamens and pistils, create a visualization that appears to emerge from a star. The design showcases the botanical intricacy and natural elegance of the bloom.

## Meaning and Philosophy

The beauty of the Bungur flower reminds us of the original nature of Surabayans—open and egalitarian while maintaining the distinctiveness of their cultural traditions. The motif conveys multiple interconnected meanings:

- **Solidarity and Tolerance**: The petals blooming together symbolize the beauty of solidarity and tolerance within the community
- **Divine Blessing and Gratitude**: The strong trunk that supports the flowers signifies the great blessings bestowed by the Creator. This represents the community's expression of gratitude and appreciation
- **Connection to Heritage**: The plant reflects the original character of those who cultivate it and represents how natural heritage adapts and remains relevant through modern times

## Cultural Context

Beyond its aesthetic value, the Bungur plant has practical significance as the community uses it as a traditional medicine and alternative remedy, demonstrating the harmonious integration of nature within daily life. This motif celebrates both the tangible and intangible values of natural heritage—aesthetic beauty, medicinal utility, and cultural continuity—that characterize Surabayan identity.
