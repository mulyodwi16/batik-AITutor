# Kampung Batik Jetis, Sidoarjo

## Location

Sidoarjo is one of the cities with economic potential for producing batik crafts. Located in East Java, it represents an important center for traditional batik production.

## Historical Background

Jetis Batik Village has existed since 1675. According to local history, Mbah Mulyadi, a descendant of the King of Kediri, fled to Sidoarjo while being pursued by the Dutch. He motivated the residents of Jetis to develop batik-making culture into a sustainable economic activity, establishing the foundation for what would become a thriving batik production community.

## Government Recognition

The local government officially inaugurated Jetis Batik Village as a traditional Sidoarjo batik production area on May 3, 2008. This inauguration aimed to introduce and promote Jetis batik more widely and to formalize its status as a cultural heritage site.

## Cultural Role

Batik production is one of the important economic activities for local residents and serves as a vital component of community identity. The village has maintained its traditional batik-making practices across generations.

## Community and Economy

Batik artisans are the main livelihood of many residents in Jetis. Batik production is both a cultural heritage and a source of income for the community, providing economic sustainability while preserving traditional craftsmanship.

## Community Leadership

**Ketua Paguyuban** (Head of the Community Association): Zainal Afandi serves as the leader of Kampung Batik Jetis Sidoarjo, overseeing the preservation and development of batik traditions within the village community.
