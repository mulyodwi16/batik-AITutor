# Kampung Batik Putat Jaya, Surabaya

## Location

Putat Jaya is a neighborhood in Surabaya located in East Java that has undergone significant urban transformation, representing an important cultural and economic revitalization initiative by the Surabaya city government.

## Historical Background

Kampung Batik Putat Jaya was not originally established as a batik artisan village. Instead, it was historically known as **Gang Dolly** (Dolly Alley), a famous localization zone in Surabaya. The community was historically characterized by its social service role rather than economic production.

In 2016, the Surabaya City Government initiated a comprehensive revitalization program to transform Gang Dolly into a craft center (**Kampung Sentra Kerajinan**). This transformation aimed to repurpose the area into creative economic sectors, preserve cultural heritage through craft production, create sustainable livelihood opportunities for residents, and establish training and mentoring centers for various crafts. The revitalization was met with strong community support, particularly for the batik craft center development.

## Government Recognition

The Surabaya City Government officially established Rumah Batik (Batik Training Center) in Putat Jaya as part of the urban revitalization initiative in 2016. This recognition aimed to formalize the status of batik production as both a cultural heritage preservation activity and a livelihood development center, distinguishing it from primarily industrial production centers.

## Cultural Role

Unlike other craft centers in the revitalization program, the batik craft center (**Rumah Batik**) serves primarily as a training and mentoring facility for batik UKMs (Small and Medium Enterprises) and a preservation hub for original Surabaya batik motifs. The center is not primarily an industrial production site, but rather a capacity-building and cultural preservation facility.

Batik production is both a cultural heritage activity and a source of income for the community, providing capacity building while preserving traditional craftsmanship. The center attracts university students, community organizations, and external visitors seeking to learn about batik production techniques and design principles.

## Community and Economy

Batik production serves as an important livelihood for residents of Putat Jaya. Residents with existing batik UKMs receive mentoring and production guidance from the craft center. Many batik UKM products are distributed to souvenir centers throughout Surabaya, creating sustainable income sources for artisans.

The batik craft center facilitates knowledge transfer among artisans and new learners, ensuring continuity of batik cultural knowledge to new generations. The transformation of Putat Jaya exemplifies how urban revitalization can balance social responsibility with economic development, preserving cultural traditions while creating contemporary opportunities for community members.

## Community Leadership

**Kepala Sentra Batik** (Head of Batik Craft Center): **Pak Sutrisna** oversees the Rumah Batik Putat Jaya, coordinates with local UKMs, manages training programs, and collaborates with the Surabaya city government on batik preservation and development initiatives.
