# Alun-Alun Contong Motif

## Origin

This motif commemorates a momentous period in 1945 at Alun-Alun Contong when heroic events occurred during the Indonesian independence struggle.

## Visual Characteristics

The motif features a distinctive cone-like shape, visually representing two pathways converging and narrowing into one, much like a funnel or tapering form. This geometric design creates a powerful visual metaphor for convergence and unity.

## Meaning and Philosophy

The Alun-Alun Contong motif symbolizes courage, sacrifice, and national pride. It represents the willingness to fight for freedom and the unification of diverse forces toward a common goal.

## Cultural Context

This motif commemorates the momentous period in 1945 when two major events occurred at Alun-Alun Contong between the youth of the Indonesian Republic and British soldiers during the struggle for Indonesian independence. To honor these heroic and sacrifice-filled moments, a monument was erected at this historical site—a statue of a freedom fighter (pejuang) carrying a sharpened bamboo spear (bambu runcing), symbolizing the courage and determination of those who fought for the nation's freedom.

Wearing this motif is an act of remembrance and respect for the martyrs and heroes who sacrificed for Indonesian independence.
