# Batik (General Overview)

## UNESCO Recognition
Batik was recognized by UNESCO on October 2, 2009, as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. This acknowledgment highlighted batik's deep cultural meaning, traditional craftsmanship, and its role as a symbol of Indonesian identity.

## National Batik Day
Since UNESCO's recognition, October 2 has been celebrated as National Batik Day in Indonesia to honor, preserve, and promote batik as a national cultural heritage.

## Cultural Significance
Batik represents Indonesian identity, traditional artistry, philosophical values, and local wisdom passed down through generations.

# Kampung Batik Jetis – Sidoarjo

## Location and Economic Role
Sidoarjo is one of the cities with economic potential for producing batik crafts. Batik production is one of the important economic activities for local residents.

## Historical Background
Jetis Batik Village has existed since 1675. According to local history, Mbah Mulyadi, a descendant of the King of Kediri, fled to Sidoarjo while being pursued by the Dutch. He motivated the residents of Jetis to develop batik-making culture into a sustainable economic activity.

## Government Recognition
The local government officially inaugurated Jetis Batik Village as a traditional Sidoarjo batik production area on May 3, 2008. This inauguration aimed to introduce and promote Jetis batik more widely.

## Community Livelihood
Batik artisans are the main livelihood of many residents in Jetis. Batik production is both a cultural heritage and a source of income for the community.

## Community Leadership
**Ketua Paguyuban** (Head of the Community Association): Zainal Afandi serves as the leader of Kampung Batik Jetis Sidoarjo, overseeing the preservation and development of batik traditions within the village community.


## Batik Motifs from Kampung Batik Jetis, Sidoarjo

### Liris Motif

#### Meaning and Philosophy
The Liris motif represents steadfastness and the ability to endure life's hardships. It conveys the teaching that one must remain strong and patient even amidst difficult circumstances—whether facing poverty, rain, or sweltering heat.

For newlyweds and newly established households, this motif holds special significance. It symbolizes the courage and willingness of partners to live humbly together and face adversity without complaint. All obstacles and challenges must be confronted and resolved together as one family unit.

---

### Alun-Alun Contong Motif

#### Visual Characteristics
The motif features a cone-like shape, visually representing two pathways converging and narrowing into one, much like a funnel or tapering form.

#### Historical Significance
This motif commemorates a momentous period in 1945 when two major events occurred at Alun-Alun Contong between the youth of the Indonesian Republic and British soldiers during the struggle for Indonesian independence. To honor these heroic and sacrifice-filled moments, a monument was erected at this historical site—a statue of a freedom fighter (pejuang) carrying a sharpened bamboo spear (bambu runcing), symbolizing the courage and determination of those who fought for the nation's freedom.

---

### Burung Merak (Peacock) Motif

#### Visual Characteristics
The Burung Merak (Peacock) motif is distinctive for its representation of a peacock displaying its long and beautiful tail feathers in full splendor.

#### Meaning and Philosophy
The peacock, recognized as one of the most exotic and magnificent birds in the world, serves as a symbol of timeless and eternal beauty. Those who wear this motif are associated with refined elegance, grace, and enduring charm that transcends time and fashion.

---

### Sekar Jagad Motif

#### Meaning
Sekar Jagad embodies the concept of beauty and diversity that captivates and enchants all those who behold it.

#### Etymology
The name "Sekar Jagad" is derived from a combination of two languages:
- "KAART" (Dutch) = map
- "JAGAD" (Javanese) = world

#### Symbolism
This motif symbolizes the remarkable diversity and richness of batik traditions found throughout Indonesia and across the entire world. It represents the concept of unity in diversity, celebrating the varied cultural heritage and different batik expressions that exist globally while remaining connected through shared artistic traditions.

---

### Parang Jabon Motif

#### Visual Characteristics
The Parang Jabon batik motif is characterized by three bright color elements: bright red, yellow, and bone white. The design features the distinctive parang (diagonal slash) pattern.

#### Meaning and Philosophy
The Parang motif carries an important message—never surrender, much like the ocean waves that never cease their movement. This pattern symbolizes constant perseverance and unending resistance.

The motif also represents unbroken connections and continuous bonds, embodying multiple meanings:
- Efforts toward self-improvement and personal growth (upaya memperbaiki diri)
- The struggle for communal welfare and prosperity (upaya memperjuangkan kesejahteraan)
- The bonds and interconnectedness of family relationships (bentuk pertalian keluarga)

---

### Love Putihan Motif

#### Visual Characteristics
The Love Putihan batik motif is depicted with a design resembling the heart symbol (love symbol). It is specially rendered with bright, cheerful colors that make the batik cloth appear vibrant and joyful.

#### Meaning and Philosophy
The heart shape combined with the bright, cheerful colors symbolizes love, happiness, and warmth of spirit. This motif is inherently associated with and identified by its heart symbolism, and reflects a joyful, positive, and optimistic spiritual attitude.

# Batik Motifs from Surabaya

## Kintir Kintiran Motif

### Creator
Nuraini Farida

### Design Concept and Meaning
This batik motif is named to represent Surabaya, which is surrounded by several rivers with abundant and clean natural water resources free from pollution. It also symbolizes that Arek Suroboyo (the spirit of Surabayans) is like flowing water that can adapt wherever the tide of progress flows. In essence, the people of Surabaya can endure any condition to pursue their livelihood and aspirations.

### Ornamental Elements

**Jagged Line Ornament**
Represents the natural richness of mangrove ecosystems with their sharp, intertwined roots.

**Suro and Boyo Ornaments**
Symbolizes Arek Suroboyo spirit. The semanggi (water clover) plant, a water plant, represents love and harmony.

**Bamboo Line Ornaments**
Symbolizes struggle (perjuangan) and loyalty (loyalitas).

### Original Inspiration Source
The rivers flowing through the city of Surabaya, including mangrove ecosystems with all their advantages and natural richness.

### Philosophy
Arek Suroboyo, known for their toughness, possess loyalty and love that harmonizes with their spirit of struggle (semangat berjuang).

---

## Gembili Wonokromo Motif

### Creator
Wahyu Subiyantoro

### Location and Context
Gembili, Gadung, and Bendul Merisi represent a middle-class neighborhood that embodies "Little Surabaya," with residents from all social strata living together. Located on the outskirts of the city during the 1970s, this area was relatively bustling due to the presence of Wonokromo Market, a nursing school, and the RSAL (Naval Hospital) facilities.

### Daytime Atmosphere
The mornings are busy and vibrant with life. Small children head off to school, traveling vendors move in and out of the neighborhood alleys, and occasional sounds from Jalan A. Yani and the sirens of ambulances drift through the area.

### Nighttime Atmosphere
The evenings in Gembili have a unique charm. Like villages throughout Surabaya, Gembili's nights are not silent. Elderly residents and retirees gather in clusters for conversations that extend into the night, and the faint sounds of "klenengan" (traditional Javanese musical gatherings) or "Ludruk" (traditional Javanese folk theater) drift softly from neighborhood radios through the quiet late-night hours.

### Meaning and Purpose
The Gembili Wonokromo batik motif attempts to capture and express the memories of this distinctive community atmosphere—both the bustling daytime energy and the peaceful, culturally rich nighttime character of this neighborhood.

## Kembang Bungur Motif

### Creator
Risha Iffatur Rahmah

### Visual Characteristics
The Bungur flower motif features the distinctive shape of the Bungur blossom (also known as Ketangi, Laban, or Wungu). The wavy petals of the flower calyx, supported by the delicate stamens and pistils, create a visualization that appears to emerge from a star.

### Meaning and Philosophy

**Character and Values of Surabaya**
The beauty of the Bungur flower reminds us of the original nature of Surabayans—open and egalitarian while maintaining the distinctiveness of their cultural traditions.

**Solidarity and Tolerance**
The petals blooming together symbolize the beauty of solidarity and tolerance within the community.

**Divine Blessing and Gratitude**
The strong trunk that supports the flowers signifies the great blessings bestowed by the Creator. This represents the community's expression of gratitude and appreciation.

**Connection to Indonesian Flora**
The Bungur is part of Indonesia's native flora and is commonly found throughout Surabaya. This plant reflects the original character of those who cultivate it and represents how natural heritage adapts and remains relevant through modern times.

**Community Utility**
Beyond its aesthetic value, the plant has practical significance as the community uses it as a traditional medicine and alternative remedy, demonstrating the harmonious integration of nature within daily life.

## Sparkling Motif

### Creator
Arina Halimatul Anjani

### Name and Inspiration
The batik motif "Sparkling: Taste of Surabaya" is inspired by the diversity of arts, culture, and cuisine unique to the city of Surabaya.

### Design Elements

**Sparkling (Dance Element)**
The word "Sparkling" is derived from Surabaya's distinctive dance art form: Tari Sparkling (Sparkling Dance). This dance represents a fusion of modern and traditional dance movements, symbolizing beauty, spirit, and the essence of Arek Suroboyo (Surabayan people). The dance is characterized by nimble, graceful movements that are rich with aesthetic beauty.

**Taste (Culinary Element)**
The word "Taste" (referring to flavor/palate) is inspired by Surabaya's signature dish: Rujak Cingur. This traditional delicacy consists of vegetables, tofu, tempeh, and cingur (cow's nose), which is beloved by the people of Surabaya. The dish represents local culinary heritage and community taste.

### Meaning and Philosophy
The selection of these two inspirational sources—Sparkling Dance and Rujak Cingur—reflects and represents the spirit and semangat of Arek Suroboyo (Surabayan people). This spirit originates from the rich diversity of arts and cuisine within Surabayan society, showcasing how cultural and culinary traditions together embody the identity and values of the community.

## Remo Suroboyoan Motif

### Creator
Umi Badriyah

### Art Form Inspiration
This batik motif is inspired by one of Surabaya's distinctive art forms: Tari Remo (Remo Dance), a traditional dance that represents the spirit and character of Surabayan people.

### Design Concept and Symbolism

**Speed and Agility**
The motif captures the swift and agile hand movements characteristic of Tari Remo, reflecting the quick reflexes and dexterity required in this dynamic dance form.

**Unity of Inner Strength**
The design symbolizes the unification of all the strength and power within a person's inner self, representing wholeness and inner harmony.

**Courage and Struggle**
The motif embodies the spirit of a person engaged in battle on the battlefield with valor and courage. This represents the unwavering determination and bravery of Arek Suroboyo in facing life's challenges and struggles with dignity and strength.

## Abhi Boyo Motif

### Creator
Heri Supriyanto

### Etymology and Character

**Name Meaning**
The name "Abhi Boyo" derives from the Sanskrit: "Abhi Manyu," where:
- **Abhi** = Berani / Pemberani (Brave / Courageous)
- **Manyu** = Tabiat (Nature / Character)

**Abhi Manyu Character**
An Abhi Manyu represents someone who is fearless and embodies heroic qualities. This person possesses the following characteristics:
- Refined and gentle demeanor (halus)
- Virtuous conduct and good behavior
- Clear and honest speech (ucapannya terang)
- Strong and resolute heart (hatinya keras)
- Great sense of responsibility
- Courageous and brave spirit

### Symbolic Elements

**Buaya (Crocodile)**
The crocodile (Boyo) represents a fierce and frightening creature—a formidable enemy that threatens life and safety. It symbolizes the grave dangers and challenges one must confront.

**Parang Motif**
The Parang motif conveys a message to never surrender, much like the ocean waves that never cease their movement. It represents perpetual resistance and unending determination.

**Interlocking Motif**
The continuous, unbroken interlocking patterns symbolize:
- Efforts at self-improvement (upaya memperbaiki diri)
- The struggle for communal welfare and prosperity
- The bonds and interconnectedness of family (bentuk pertalian keluarga)

### Historical and Cultural Narrative

**The Legend**
This motif tells the story of a heroic figure with refined and virtuous qualities who faces an enemy as strong and terrifying as a crocodile. Originally, the region was peaceful, cool, and serene. However, territorial disputes arise, leading to conflict and death.

**Connection to Arek Suroboyo**
The young people of Surabaya, embodying the same brave and unwavering spirit, rose to defend their territory against the invading Tar-Tar forces. Despite facing formidable danger, Raden Wijaya was protected and ultimately survived the assault and threats.

**Surabaya's Birthday**
The day of this victorious struggle was commemorated as Surabaya's birthday, celebrated on May 31. This date represents not only a military victory but also the eternal spirit of Arek Suroboyo—brave, steadfast, and willing to sacrifice for their homeland and community.
